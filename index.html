Section 2 — Body

Three Core Arguments

Each argument builds the case that AI is a powerful ally for students — when approached with intention.

📚
Argument 01
AI Helps Students Learn Faster

AI explains difficult topics in simple language. Students can ask questions as many times as needed — without embarrassment. It provides examples, exercises, and personalized explanations, making learning faster and more accessible for everyone.

Many educational platforms already use AI to personalize learning. Students receive explanations adapted to their own pace — meaning they aren't left behind or held back by a one-size-fits-all curriculum.

💼
Argument 02
AI Prepares Students for Future Jobs

AI is becoming an essential tool in nearly every profession. Businesses already use AI to improve productivity and decision-making. Learning to work alongside AI today directly prepares students for the careers of tomorrow.

Companies in engineering, business, medicine, marketing, education, and technology are already integrating AI into daily operations. Students with demonstrated AI skills will have significantly more professional opportunities.

💡
Argument 03
AI Improves Creativity & Problem Solving

AI does not replace creativity — it amplifies it. It helps students generate ideas, organize projects, improve their writing, and solve problems more efficiently. Students still make all the final decisions; AI simply removes friction.

Many educators now actively encourage responsible AI use for brainstorming, research, and feedback — treating it as a thinking partner rather than a shortcut. The key is teaching students when and how to engage with it.

Section 3 — Counterargument

Addressing the Opposition

Every strong argument must engage honestly with the other side.

❌ Opposite Opinion
AI Encourages Cheating & Laziness

Some people believe students should not use Artificial Intelligence because it encourages cheating and makes students lazy. They worry students will stop thinking for themselves and simply copy AI-generated answers without truly learning.

VS
✅ Stronger Response
The Problem Is How, Not What

Artificial Intelligence is only a tool. The problem is not the technology itself — it is how people choose to use it. Just like calculators and the Internet, AI can be used responsibly or irresponsibly. Instead of banning AI, schools should teach students how to use it ethically. Responsible use creates better learners, not lazy students.

Introduction

Attention Grabber

Imagine finishing your homework faster while understanding every topic better. Artificial Intelligence is already changing the way we study, work, and communicate. Every day, millions of students use AI to solve problems, learn faster, and improve their education. So, here is my question: Should students use Artificial Intelligence, or should they avoid it?

Link to the Audience

As university students, we all want better grades, more free time, and better opportunities after graduation. Artificial Intelligence is becoming part of our daily lives, so learning how to use it affects every one of us.

Credentials

I became interested in this topic because AI tools are already transforming education around the world. After researching and using these tools myself, I discovered that they can improve learning when they are used responsibly.

Goal

Today I want to convince you that students should learn to use Artificial Intelligence responsibly instead of fearing it.

Main Points

During this speech I will explain: how AI helps students learn faster; how AI prepares students for future careers; and why responsible use is better than banning AI.

Body — Argument 1: AI Helps Students Learn Faster

Artificial Intelligence explains difficult topics in simple language. Students can ask questions as many times as they need without feeling embarrassed. AI provides examples, exercises, and personalized explanations. Learning becomes faster and more accessible.

Evidence: Many educational platforms already use AI to personalize learning. Students receive explanations adapted to their own pace.

Body — Argument 2: AI Prepares Students for Future Jobs

Artificial Intelligence is becoming an important tool in almost every profession. Businesses already use AI to improve productivity. Learning to work with AI today prepares students for tomorrow's careers.

Evidence: Companies in engineering, business, medicine, marketing, education, and technology are already using Artificial Intelligence. Students with AI skills will have more professional opportunities.

Body — Argument 3: AI Improves Creativity and Problem Solving

Artificial Intelligence does not replace creativity. Instead, it helps students generate ideas, organize projects, improve writing, and solve problems more efficiently. Students continue making the final decisions.

Evidence: Many teachers encourage responsible AI use for brainstorming, research, and feedback instead of copying answers.

Counterargument

Some people believe students should not use Artificial Intelligence because it encourages cheating and makes students lazy. They worry students will stop thinking for themselves.

However, Artificial Intelligence is only a tool. The problem is not the technology. The problem is how people choose to use it. Just like calculators and the Internet, AI can be used responsibly or irresponsibly. Instead of banning AI, schools should teach students how to use it ethically. Responsible use creates better learners, not lazy students.

Conclusion

Artificial Intelligence helps students learn faster. It prepares us for future jobs. It also improves creativity when used responsibly. Instead of fearing AI, we should learn how to use it wisely.

Call to Action

Don't be afraid of Artificial Intelligence. Start learning how to use it responsibly. Use it to ask questions. Use it to improve your ideas. Use it to become a better student. The future belongs to those who know how to work with technology.

Thank you.
